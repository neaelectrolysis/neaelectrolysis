** The concern for endometrial cancer in PCOS is due to irregular or absent periods. If the uterine lining isnâ€™t shedding often enough, this increases risk for endometrial cancer.

A hormonal contraceptive containing a progestin (synthetic progesterone) is recommended when a certain threshold for irregularity in menses is met, for individuals with an intact uterus. Such as a progestin-only pill (POP); or combination birth control (COCP) which contains both a progestin & ethinyl estradiol; or non-pill forms such as the IUD, patch, shot (most of which are progestin-only).

Ethinyl estradiol, found in hormonal contraceptives, isnâ€™t necessary for reducing risk of endometrial cancer; However, this is the primary synthetic hormone in this drug class that helps with managing hyperandrogenism in PCOS.

There are a variety of progestins available for hormonal contraceptives. Most progestins have varying androgenic effects; but at this time, no particular hormonal contraceptive is recommended due to lack of evidence-based data on the clinical implications of different progestins for individuals with hyperandrogenism or PCOS. Drospirenone is available in COCPs and as a POP (Slynd). This is the only progestin available in a progestin-only pill that has mild anti-androgenic properties.
